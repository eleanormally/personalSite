Book Log

This is something I've been wanting to record so that I can keep track of my thoughts and reference it as a resource for recommendations. Perhaps other content logs are in my future, who knows! I'll try to provide a link to the book, but will avoid linking out to booksellers directly.

  • Algospeak, by Adam Aleksic

    I enjoyed this book well enough. It had been a while since I had read much in the world of linguistics. I was first introduced to Aleksic via his short-form videos (@etymologynerd), and when reading this book I found that much of his short-form style came through, both for good and bad. The reading is easy but ultimately left me hungry for more depth and nuance. As a certified linguistics enjoyer, I was particularly intrigued by the sociolinguistics of "permission" as it came to out-groups using in-group language and thus dispersing it and changing the meaning. Growing up when this kind of lexical dispersion was commonplace, I hadn't thought of the the social pressures discouraging use of an in-group's words if you were not in that context. Certainly in the category of pop linguistics, but a fun read.

    3/5

    finished: around June 2026

  • Death's End, by 刘慈欣 (liú cí xīn)

    I read this book over the course of about a year, and wow do I wish that I had read it more quickly. This is the third installment of the "3 Body Problem" series, of which I thoroughly enjoyed books 1 and 2 (The 3 Body Problem and The Dark Forest) respectively. Unfortunately, in between my finishing of The Dark Forest and starting Death's End, I watched season 1 of Netflix's adaptation of the series. This takes a more strictly chronological approach than the books, and when I got into the start of Death's End I was less enthused after realizing the first section of the book was a significant portion of the Netflix adaptation (which I did not care for). Once I I got beyond that, however, this was a fascinating read. 刘's writing intertwines interesting science fiction with equally enticing philosophy and thoughts on the Fermi Paradox, and I look forward to reading more of his work. I will make not that I read these books in English (at some point I may try again in the original Chinese), and I preferred the translation style of Ken Liu in books 1 and 3 to that of Joel Martinsen in book 2.

    4.5/5

    finished: around March 2026
